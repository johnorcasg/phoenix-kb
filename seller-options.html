Seller Options – Comprehensive Guide

Version 1.0 • Updated 2025-09-12 • Phoenix Inbound KB

Principles

Gather facts; no promises; plain English; no legal/tax advice. Required: Motivation, Condition, Timeline, Price; plus loan/payoff/arrears/HOA/liens/tenant context when relevant.

Cash

  • Simple, fast, as-is; lower price vs retail; need access timing and repair scope.

Subject-To (Subto)

  • Buyer continues seller’s existing mortgage; title transfers.
  • Pros: can pay more; faster than refi. Cons: due-on-sale risk; servicing discipline required.
  • Collect: lender/servicer, rate, balance, PITI, arrears, escrow/tax/insurance status, HOA.

Wrap

  • New note to seller wraps existing note; buyer pays seller; seller pays lender.
  • Pros: flexible terms; Cons: compliance/servicing risk.
  • Collect: underlying terms, desired net/monthly, DP, balloon/term/interest prefs.

Hybrid

  • Mix of cash + terms to bridge gaps; define cash now, monthly, term/balloon tolerances.

Seller Carry

  • Seller finances buyer directly; potential highest net over time; collect price, DP, rate/term, prepay/balloon, servicing approach.

AUX: Foreclosure, Liens, Condition, Tenants

  • Foreclosure: sale date, arrears, NOD/NTS, utilities, code issues.
  • Liens: IRS/state/municipal/HOA/mechanics; payoff estimates if known.
  • Condition: roof/HVAC/plumbing/foundation, permits, flood/claims.
  • Tenants: lease terms, arrears/cooperation, notice feasiblity, cash-for-keys.

Safe language

“I’m gathering details so our team can see what could fit. Nothing is committed today.”

Operational knowledge only. No guarantees, no legal/tax advice.
